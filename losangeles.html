California: Music & Palm Trees

  • 17 Feb 2019
  • By Ryan Drew
  • Travel

Travel

My travels began on Thursday at 3:00AM in Charlottetown—there really is no fun way to do international travel from the far East coast of Canada! PEI’s only commercial airport serves mainly to connect travelers to Canada’s larger airports such as Toronto Pearson and Montréal-Pierre Elliott Trudeau, meaning that catching a morning flight of out of central Canada requires an adventure of its own well before sunrise. Fortunately, all went as well as it could, and I arrived in California later that morning.

This was my first time in California and I’d be lying if I said I didn’t have some expectations of what the sunny Southern state would be like to explore!

Music Making

Los Angeles’s music industry accounts for a significant part of the city-wide media production hotspot, and is central to the global music economy. Coming from an academic background in classical music, experiencing this vibrant music scene first hand is both inspiring and refreshing—a world that I am only beginning to become familiar with. Visiting with music producer and long-time friend Julien Lopes, we were able to sit down and record a few tunes on one of California’s uncharacteristically rainy days. One of the tracks I am featured in can be heard in Julien’s recent video, found here! Pursuing an exciting career in music making, Julien is currently living with fellow producers Joe and Hamps.

"Un-blocking" Creative Barriers

The topic of creative block came up in a discussion I had with Joe. Specifically, we talked about how creative block affects idea development and improvisation at the piano. This is a barrier that I often find myself at, regularly sitting down to mess around with the same general ideas, chord progressions, and voice leading. This is a “block” that many music creators know all to well, and I would suggest that a lot of it is in your hands rather than your head—it’s a mechanical limitation. You can become so increasingly comfortable with a refined set of hand placements and movements that you become very narrow minded in your creative habits. How can you overcome this? Well, one solution is to better develop a fluency working in all key signatures and modes, and expand your technical and stylistic abilities through regular exercises. Sounds fun, right? Well, the more accessible, and perhaps more constructive solution to this form of creative block is to listen, watch, and imitate other musicians. I find that this approach can help to trigger new perspectives and understandings, and can play a big part in “un-blocking” your creative restraints.

Life in California

Circulating tens of thousands of tourists each day, spending a day walking down Venice Beach was definitely worth the hype. I was immediately captivated by how the diverse architectural styles populating the area are unified by simple, pastel color tones. Local infrastructure and streets share a rustic grunginess, palm trees and other exotic vegetation line the sidewalks, and much like the rest of California, the area promotes an aura of 80s and 90s pop-culture. In stark contrast with this refined visual aesthetic, the greater Los Angeles area exists on a digital plane that is unmatched in today’s word. A general sense of familiarity with the area can be felt from the moment one steps out of their Uber—the coast’s digital presence is both powerful and global, finding its way onto the average Instagram feed on a daily basis. Pointed out by one member of our group that you can reduce most of the beach’s regulars into a handful of categories: the vloggers, the bodybuilders, the skater crowd, the Fortnight kids, and the homeless. It’s an eclectic environment that generates (and consumes) media at exorbitant rates.

Live Music

Attending Oliver Tree’s performance at The Roxy was one of the highlights of my trip. Admittedly, I wasn’t overly familiar with his work prior to the show, and now looking back on the show, there really is no easy way to summarize the eccentric performance. Tree’s on-stage persona reflects the image he has cultivated throughout his steady rise in internet fame. Featuring a signature bowl cut, Fuck Jerry branded clothing, and a vast repertoire of bizarre, spastic dance movements, Oliver Tree has carefully crafted an identity that he truly shares with no one else. Despite all of this, his music is tasteful, aligning with common conventions and regularities of today’s pop and hip-hop genres. His work is both well-structured and catchy, and his stage presence—albeit strange—is exceptional. If you haven’t already, be sure to check him out!

Palm Trees

Like many, I have always thought of palm trees as a symbol for California life—and as it turns out, that’s exactly why they’re there! Admittedly, I was probably more excited by the local assortment of palm trees then most people would be… However, it turns out their history in the state is rather interesting. Only one species of palm trees is actually native to the land, with most of what we see today imported in the late 19th century. Why? In an effort to establish an identity and grow as a society, the trees were brought to the area for aesthetic purposes. Unfortunately, environmental scientists predict that as a result of destructive insects and disease, Los Angeles’s palm tree population will be significantly reduced in the decades to come.
